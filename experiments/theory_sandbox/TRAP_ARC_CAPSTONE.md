## 1. The capstone finding
For the externally-canonical **Conway glider (B3/S23)**, the **passive local rule-mask trap paradigm is exhausted** for **identity-preserving trapping**.

Across four independent framings — *over-bind*, *block*, *lock*, and *confine* — no passive, spatially-fixed, local rule-mask (a sector running a modified update rule; **no global clock, no placed Life objects, no non-local topology, no active control**) both (a) arrests or bounds a canonical glider **and** (b) preserves its identity as a recognizable translating glider. Every framing collapses into a characterized failure mode, or it leaks out of the rule-mask class into a *different primitive* (placed-object reflector, topology/wrap, or active timing) that the paradigm explicitly excludes.

**Root cause (the load-bearing insight, reached from three directions): a glider's identity *is* its motion.** It has **no held-rest state** to lock-and-preserve. So "stop it / pen it" and "keep it a glider" are near-contradictory for this target under a *bulk rule change* — and steering a glider while keeping it intact (reflect/turn) is a **catalyzed reaction that requires an engineered placed object**, not a region of altered law. A rule-mask can *block*, *over-bind*, or *pass* — it cannot supply the delicate catalysis that identity-preserving capture or reflection demands.

This is the **capture↔identity tension**, now confirmed from three angles: **freeze** (Toy #5 v1), **compliance** (Toy #6), and **confinement** (feasibility memo). It is a strong convergent result, not a single data point.

### 2a. Confine-not-lock feasibility — durable record of the read-only finding
The confine-not-lock idea was assessed **read-only** (chat-only feasibility memo, no script, no repo work) and is recorded here so the finding survives in the repo.

- **Idea:** preserve a glider's identity by **bounding** its motion (reflect/contain it in a "soft box") rather than stopping it — identity preserved *because* motion is preserved.
- **Candidates and their fates:** *(a) rule-mask boundary reflection* — a uniform modified rule provides no engineered catalyst; reflection in Life is a catalyzed reaction, so a glider entering a modified-rule region generically chaos/dies/transforms at the rule-discontinuity. *(b) sector-internal "refractive" rule* — the same 5-cell pattern is generally not a clean glider under a different rule, so the interface shreds or transforms it; discrete CA has no smooth "bending." *(c) topology / periodic-wrap* — a torus genuinely bounds a circulating glider with identity intact, **but wrap is non-local connectivity, not a local rule-mask** → a *different primitive* (out of class). *(d) gradient / "potential well"* — gliders do not respond to fields; a scalar field does not touch Life dynamics → no effect / pass-through.
- **Decisive reason:** known Life glider reflectors are **placed patterns** (engineered still-lifes/oscillators acting as catalysts), **not rule masks**. A uniform per-cell rule change supplies no catalyst → no clean reflection → "needs placed object" leakage.
- **Verdict:** collapses analytically (like lock-as-framed), or reduces to an out-of-class primitive. A reasoned assessment from known Life behavior, **not a proof**; some exotic rule-mask cannot be categorically excluded, but the weight of Life knowledge points firmly to collapse.
